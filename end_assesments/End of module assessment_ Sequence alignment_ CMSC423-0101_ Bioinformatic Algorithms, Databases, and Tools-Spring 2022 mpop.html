    Describe an algorithm that computes the fitting alignment between two strings S1 and S2.  Specifically, we are trying to identify a substring of S1 , such that the alignment between the entirety of S2 and this substring has the largest score over all possible choices of substrings from S1.

    Please describe your algorithm in terms of how you would modify the local or global alignment algorithms.  Specifically, please address the following 4 questions:

    1. What values will you initialize in the first row and column of the matrix?

    2. Will you use the local or global variant of the recurrence function (i.e., will you allow free rides or not)?

    3. Where does backtracking start?

    4. Where does backtracking end?

    Your Answer:

    1. in the first row we would initialize with 0 and first column we would have gap penalties of 1 

    2. There would be no free rides 

    3. backtracking still starts at the top right corner of the matrix 

    4. backtrack ends at returns at the highest score found. 

     

    You would have to calculate the score penalties just like we did for global alignment except that the penalty is only 1. and follow the path until the highest score is found using recurrence equations. 

    backtracking cannot start from top right.
